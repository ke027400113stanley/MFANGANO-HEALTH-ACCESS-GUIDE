    2. DROWNING

    First aid for drowning involves ensuring your safety, getting the person out of the water, and immediately starting CPR if they are not breathing normally. Call emergency services, tilt the head back to open the airway, and give 5 rescue breaths followed by 30 chest compressions. Repeat until help arrives or the person starts breathing.

    • Ensure Your Safety & Get Help

    Do not enter dangerous water. If safe, throw a rope or extend a pole. Call for emergency services immediately.

    • Move the Person & Check Response

    Get them out of the water onto a firm surface. Check consciousness by tapping and shouting.

    • Open the Airway

    Gently tilt the head back and lift the chin to open the airway.

    • Give Rescue Breaths

    Pinch the nose, seal your mouth over theirs, and give 5 slow rescue breaths.

    • Start Chest Compressions

    Push hard and fast at the center of the chest, 100–120 compressions per minute, 2 inches deep.

    • Continue CPR

    Cycle 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives or the person recovers.

    • After the Person Recovers

    Place in recovery position, remove wet clothes, keep warm, and seek medical care to prevent secondary drowning.
